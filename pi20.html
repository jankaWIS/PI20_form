Introduction to Questionnaire

In this questionnaire we would like to ask you to answer the following 20 short questions. In each there will be a scale from 1 (strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree). Please answer all questions.

Q2: I have always had a bad memory for faces.






Q3: I find it notably easier to recognize people who have distinctive facial features.






Q4: I often mistake people I have met before for strangers.






Q5: When I was at school I struggled to recognize my classmates.






Q6: When people change their hairstyle, or wear hats, I have problems recognizing them.






Q7: I sometimes have to warn new people I meet that I am ‘bad with faces’.






Q8: I find it easy to picture individual faces in my mind.






Q9: I am better than most people at putting a ‘name to a face’.






Q10: Without hearing people's voices, I struggle to recognize them.






Q11: Anxiety about face recognition has led me to avoid certain social or professional situations.






Q12: I have to try harder than other people to memorize faces.






Q13: I am very confident in my ability to recognize myself in photographs.






Q14: I sometimes find movies hard to follow because of difficulties recognizing characters.






Q15: My friends and family think I have bad face recognition or bad face memory.






Q16: I feel like I frequently offend people by not recognizing who they are.






Q17: It is easy for me to recognize individuals in situations that require people to wear similar clothes (e.g. suits, uniforms and swimwear).






Q18: At family gatherings, I sometimes confuse individual family members.






Q19: I find it easy to recognize celebrities in ‘before-they-were-famous’ photos, even if they have changed considerably.






Q20: It is hard to recognize familiar people when I meet them out of context (e.g. meeting a work colleague unexpectedly while shopping).
